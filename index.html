Artificial Intelligence

Where Are We Now?

Artificial Intelligence has experienced unprecedented growth in recent years, evolving from a niche academic discipline into one of the most influential forces of our time. To understand its true scope, this website explores the phenomenon through four key perspectives:

Models – The technical evolution of machine learning algorithms, with a focus on training processes.

Performance & Benchmarks – The measurable capabilities these models have developed over time.

Investments – The financial resources and market dynamics driving innovation.

Socio-Environmental Impact – The broader consequences for society and the planet.

Artificial Intelligence Models

We observe how algorithms evolve over the years, becoming increasingly larger and more computationally expensive to train.

In particular, the following aspects are analyzed: the number of model parameters, the total Floating Point Operations (FLOPs) required during training, and finally the total training hours compared with dataset size.

The evaluation of these technical characteristics forms the core of the analysis presented in this section.

Performance & Benchmark

Do a large number of parameters or longer training times necessarily lead to better models? How can we determine whether an algorithm truly performs well?

In recent years, standardized evaluation techniques have been developed to measure AI capabilities. These assessments consist of structured tasks—ranging from logical and mathematical reasoning to linguistic and social understanding—designed to test different aspects of model intelligence.

These evaluation tasks are known as benchmarks, and they form the core of the analysis presented in this section.
